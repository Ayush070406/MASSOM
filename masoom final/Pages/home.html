Disease caused in Women

AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome) AIDS stands for Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome. It is a chronic, potentially life-threatening condition caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). HIV attacks the immune system, specifically targeting CD4 cells (T cells), which are crucial for fighting off infections and diseases. As the virus destroys more CD4 cells and progresses, the body becomes more susceptible to various infections and cancers. Practice Safe Sex: Use condoms correctly and consistently during sexual intercourse, including vaginal, anal, and oral sex. This reduces the risk of HIV transmission. Limit Sexual Partners: Reducing the number of sexual partners can lower the risk of exposure to HIV. Get Tested Regularly: Knowing your HIV status is crucial. Regular testing helps in early detection and treatment, which can significantly improve outcomes.

Uterus Cancer

A major concern

Uterine cancer, also known as endometrial cancer, is a significant health concern affecting women worldwide. It arises from abnormal cell growth in the lining of the uterus, or endometrium, and can manifest in various forms, posing unique challenges for diagnosis and treatment. As awareness and early detection are paramount in combating this disease, understanding its risk factors, symptoms, and available interventions is crucial..

PCOD

We are with you.

PCOD is a condition characterized by hormonal imbalance, leading to irregular menstrual cycles, excess androgen levels (male hormones), and the development of small cysts on the ovaries. Symptoms may include irregular periods, weight gain, acne, excessive hair growth (hirsutism), and difficulty conceiving. The exact cause of PCOD is not fully understood but is believed to involve a combination of genetic, hormonal, and lifestyle factors. Insulin resistance and obesity are commonly associated with PCOD.

WORLD HEALTH ORGANISATION

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. It was established in 1948 and is headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland. The WHO's primary objective is to promote health, monitor global health trends, provide technical assistance to countries, and coordinate international responses to health emergencies. It works on various health issues, including disease prevention, health promotion, healthcare systems strengthening, and the development of international health regulations.

How Abortion works?

Abortion is a medical procedure used to end a pregnancy. The method used depends on factors like how far along the pregnancy is and the woman's health. Common methods include medication abortion (using pills to end the pregnancy) and surgical abortion (where the contents of the uterus are removed using medical instruments). It's important for individuals considering abortion to consult with healthcare professionals for accurate information and guidance tailored to their specific situation.­
